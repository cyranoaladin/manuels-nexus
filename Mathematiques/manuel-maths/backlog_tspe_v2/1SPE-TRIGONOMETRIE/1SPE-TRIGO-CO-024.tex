\textbf{1.} $\cos(2a) = 2\cos^2 a - 1 \implies \cos^2 a = \dfrac{1 + \cos(2a)}{2}$.

\textbf{2.} $\cos(2a) = 1 - 2\sin^2 a \implies \sin^2 a = \dfrac{1 - \cos(2a)}{2}$.

\textbf{3.} $\cos^2\!\left(\dfrac{\pi}{8}\right) = \dfrac{1 + \cos\!\left(\dfrac{\pi}{4}\right)}{2} = \dfrac{1 + \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}}{2} = \dfrac{2 + \sqrt{2}}{4}$.
